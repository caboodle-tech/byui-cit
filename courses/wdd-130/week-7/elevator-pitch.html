Elevator Pitch

The Final project for this class will be for you to create a website on a topic of your choice. There will also be content available for a semi-fictitious shipping company Allen Ship Center, but you are encouraged to come up with your own idea. We will start building this site next week, in order to prepare for that we need to start planning now. Over the next few class sessions we will continue to learn web fundamentals as we prepare and build a Site Plan for our final personal site.

The first step in the planning process is to determine the purpose of the site, so this week you need to settle on your idea for what you want to do. You should then create an elevator pitch for your personal site idea. Creating an elevator pitch can be a good gauge of the quality of your ideas and your enthusiasm for them.

What is an Elevator Pitch?

An elevator pitch is a brief persuasive speech that you can use to spark interest in what you or your organization does. You can also use one to create interest in a project, idea, or product. It should be succinct while still conveying important information.

Assignment:

  1. The 9 C's of Elevator Pitches

    The 9 C's of Elevator Pitches are summarized below. You should also read the full Elevator Pitch 101 essay by Chris O'Leary.

    Concise
    An effective elevator pitch contains as few words as possible, but no fewer.
    Clear
    Rather than being filled with acronyms, MBA-speak, and ten-dollar words, an effective elevator pitch can be understood by your grandparents, your spouse, and your children.
    Compelling
    An effective elevator pitch explains the problem your Solution solves.
    Credible
    An effective elevator pitch explains why you are qualified to see the problem and to build your Solution.
    Conceptual
    An effective elevator pitch stays at a fairly high level and does not go into too much unnecessary detail.
    Concrete
    As much as is possible, an effective elevator pitch is also specific and tangible.
    Customized
    An effective elevator pitch addresses the specific interests and concerns of the audience.
    Consistent
    Every version of an effective elevator pitch conveys the same basic message.
    Conversational
    Rather than being to close the deal, the goal of an elevator pitch is to just set the hook; to start a conversation, or dialogue, with the audience.

    The preceding content is an excerpt from Chris O'Leary's copyrighted essay Elevator Pitch 101. This excerpt has been included here for educational use under the Fair Use doctrine.

  2. Examples

    Consider the following examples. Can you see the application of the 9 C's in them?

    • "I work with Computers"
    • "I’m a computer programmer"
    • "I make mobile applications"
    • "My company writes mobile device applications for other businesses."
    • "My company develops mobile applications that businesses use to train their staff remotely. This results in a big increase in efficiency for an organization's managers."
    • "Think about the last online purchase you made. Did you need any instructions to do it? Did everything go smoothly? That’s what I do… I make websites and online storefronts that look great and are so intuitive to use that people don’t need instructions or training to use them. I do this for a wide variety of businesses."

  3. Create a pitch for your website idea.

    Create a 100-250 word pitch (about 60 to 90 seconds of spoken dialog) for an idea that you would like to turn into a website. You can use the following process to help:

    1. Identify your idea or goal.
    2. Explain what you want the website to do.
    3. Communicate your USP. (Unique Selling Proposition)
    4. Engage with a question
    5. Put it all together.
    6. Practice!

  4. Feedback & Publish

    Once you have put your pitch together and practiced it, share it with 3-4 people and get their feedback. These people can be anyone you think will give good feedback to you, but this sharing must be done synchronously; a face to face conversation or video conference ideally. After sharing and getting feedback upload your pitch and the feedback to the Elevator Pitch assignment in Canvas.

    Grading:

    This activity will be graded using the following rubric: A completed Elevator pitch of 100-250 words that includes feedback from others. Place the feedback in the same file as the Elevator Pitch and organize the content appropriately.

How to create your 30 second elevator pitch by Intern Queen
This video starts a bit cringey but teaches the concept of personal elevator pitches in a succinct way. This video is more about how to network, socialize, and land a job interview in a professional way. Avoid high fluctuations in your voice and sentences so fast the person can hear you gasp for air.
How To Create A Personal Elevator Pitch by Professor Heather Austin
This is a quick video that covers creating a personal elevator pitch for your use in professional environments. Notice the emphasis on smooth informative sentences that lead to a conversion instead of a 30 second one way conversation.
